## What is a network?

A network refers to two or more connected devices that share data and resources.

This can be accomplished by hardwiring devices together to what’s called a **switch**. 

![](https://i.imgur.com/FXQ5iaw.png)

**or**

You can connect your devices wirelessly through a **Wireless Access Point**.

## How Devices Communicate on a Network

The “language” that is defined for how devices will communicate over a network is called a **protocol**. Some examples of common protocols are:

**Ethernet or TCP** - These are protocols for how data is exchanged

**Http/Https** - These protocols you have seen before, they are for accessing websites

**Smtp** - Is a protocol for sending emails

Protocols are constantly working together to communicate and transfer data

### Network Sizes

**SOHO (Small Office, Home Office)**
- What most people have in their homes, a laptop, printer, phones, consoles, etc.
- Depending on your set up you could be using a router, cabling devices into a switch or a combination of the two. 

**Enterprise Network** 
 - These networks are very large and can span cities, states, even globally.

**Internet/Service Provider (ISP) Network**
- Offer services to connect different customers together. 

**LAN** - Local Area Network
- Devices that are **geographically** located on the same network. 
- This can be both a very small network of devices, all the way to an entire floor or building of devices. LANs are **not** size dependent. 

**WAN** - Wireless Area Network 
- The joining of separate LANs together. Usually done through an ISP.

## Ethernet Cables

Not all Ethernet cables are created equal so how can you choose the right Ethernet cable for your network? use the chart below to reference what would be best for you:

| Cable | Network Speed                             |
| ----- | ----------------------------------------- |
| CAT5  | 100Mb/s                                   |
| CAT5e | 1Gb/s                                     |
| CAT6  | 10Gb/s (up to 55m)                        |
| CAT6a | 10Gb/s (up to 100m)                       |
| CAT7  | 100Gb/s (up to 15m) // 40Gb/s (up to 50m) |

For most people the CAT5e is the golden standard for SOHO networks. 

The connector at the end of a CAT cable is call an **RJ45**
